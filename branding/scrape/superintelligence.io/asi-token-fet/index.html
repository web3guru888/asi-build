Utility

The FET token enables users, developers, and stakeholders to engage with the ASI Alliance ecosystem in several meaningful ways. Here’s a breakdown of its primary utilities.

Access to AI Services

Use FET in our Web3-native ASI1-mini LLM. Also use FET to access a decentralized marketplace of AI models, agents, and services—from compute to document intelligence.

Monetize AI Services

Publish your AI agents, datasets, or models into the ASI ecosystem. With agent registries, token-gated APIs, and compute-to-data flows, developers earn FET as users consume their services.

Staking for Network Security & Yield

Stake FET to secure the Fetch.ai mainnet and earn rewards. Staking supports validator infrastructure, increases network robustness, and unlocks future ecosystem benefits such as premium access tiers.

Discover Agents

Agents must register on-chain via the Almanac to become publicly discoverable. This process, which requires FET and renews every 48 hours, ensures uptime and trustworthiness across agent ecosystems. (Live on testnet, mainnet coming soon.)

Reputation & Incentives

Agent and user reputation is tracked on-chain, influencing access, discoverability, and rewards. High-performing agents may gain visibility boosts or access to gated opportunities—creating an incentive layer for quality.

Interoperability Across Chains

FET is available on Ethereum, BSC, Fetch.ai, Cosmos, and other networks via cross-chain bridges. This unlocks composability with DeFi, DAOs, and Web3 protocols beyond ASI’s core stack.

Participate in Governance

FET holders vote on ecosystem proposals—whether funding new features, approving network upgrades, or steering protocol direction. Governance tools are being unified across Alliance members under the ASI model.

AI Compute Access & Discounts

Use FET to pay for compute workloads across decentralized networks like CUDOS. Token-based pricing, staking tiers, and integration discounts enable cost-effective access to GPU, CPU, and storage resources.

Stake & Earn

Stake your tokens, join prediction markets, contribute compute, win hackathon rewards and more! Earn $FET by fueling the ASI ecosystem. Whether you’re a developer, AI engineer, trader, or data provider, there’s a way to contribute and get rewarded.

Wallets

Storing your FET tokens securely is essential. To do this effectively, you’ll need a cryptocurrency wallet, a digital tool that safely holds your public and private keys, allowing you to send, receive, and manage your FET tokens while interacting with supported blockchains.

ASI Wallet

Hot wallets are connected to the internet, providing easy and convenient access for frequent transactions or daily use. Examples include software wallets (desktop or browser-based), mobile apps, and cryptocurrency exchanges. For managing FET, the official ASI Alliance Wallet offers user-friendly hot wallet options available through mobile apps (iOS, Android) and browser extensions.

Explore ASI Wallet

Unofficial Cold Wallets

Cold wallets offer enhanced security by storing your tokens offline, away from potential online threats. Ideal for securely holding larger amounts of FET tokens, cold wallets are less convenient for daily use but are considered extremely secure. Examples of cold wallets include hardware wallets (secure physical devices that protect your private keys) and paper wallets. FET tokens are fully supported by popular hardware wallets, including Ledger and Trezor.

Governance

Through token voting and member participation, the ASI Alliance follows a transparent, decentralized governance model shaped by its global community.

The ASI Alliance unifies four pioneering AI and Web3 projects—Fetch.ai, SingularityNET, Ocean Protocol, and CUDOS—under a shared token: $FET. While all governance activity now flows through FET, each project continues to operate its own autonomous governance mechanisms, tailored to its platform and community goals.

This federated approach ensures that decentralization doesn’t just apply to the technology—but also to decision-making.

Fetch.ai Governance

Fetch.ai leads the ASI governance stack with a mature on-chain voting system.

  • All FET holders can submit and vote on proposals related to network upgrades, funding, and operational parameters.

  • Decisions pass based on token-weighted voting and minimum participation thresholds.

  • Validators also play a role, providing network security and optional vote delegation.

Fetch.ai governance continues to serve as a backbone for broader ASI token governance.

SingularityNET Governance

SingularityNET previously used AGIX for community voting, including Deep Funding and strategic decisions.

  • Today, all governance has migrated to $FET.

  • The Deep Funding program remains active, with proposals evaluated and selected via FET-based community votes.

  • Governance continues to focus on funding grassroots innovation and ecosystem development.

SingularityNET emphasizes inclusive funding mechanisms and developer-driven roadmaps.

Ocean Protocol Governance

While OceanDAO has been sunset, Ocean continues to participate in ASI governance via $FET.

  • Community contributions now route through ASI-wide initiatives, including grants and partnerships.

  • The Ocean Foundation maintains stewardship over core infrastructure and strategic alignment within the Alliance.

Ocean plays a key role in shaping decentralized data policy and marketplace standards.

CUDOS Governance

CUDOS transitioned its governance to $FET as part of the token merger and integration.

  • Proposal creation and voting are still active through their delegated proof-of-stake network.

  • Validator-managed governance continues to inform upgrades, network economics, and community campaigns.

CUDOS retains on-chain governance for infrastructure-level decisions within ASI Compute.

Each member retains its governance identity, but all decisions now consolidate under $FET.
This model ensures that:

  • Innovation can be driven from multiple fronts.

  • Localized decisions don’t hinder cross-ecosystem collaboration.

  • Token holders have meaningful participation across diverse domains of decentralized AI.

Together, these governance systems form the decentralized coordination fabric of the ASI Alliance.

History

The ASI Alliance was formed through the tokenomic merger of Fetch.ai, SingularityNET, and Ocean Protocol—uniting leading decentralized AI, data, and infrastructure efforts. CUDOS later joined as a network member, expanding the Alliance’s capabilities in decentralized compute.

The Artificial Superintelligence (ASI) Alliance was born from the tokenomic merger of Fetch.ai, SingularityNET, and Ocean Protocol—three of the most advanced teams in decentralized AI, agent-based systems, and data infrastructure. This historic unification created the largest open-source, independent organization focused on Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) development, anchored by the $FET token.

Initiated in mid-2024, the token migration began with the consolidation of AGIX and OCEAN tokens into the Fetch.ai infrastructure on Ethereum. This strategic move laid the groundwork for a cohesive, interoperable platform capable of supporting real-world, cross-domain AI use cases at scale. In a subsequent expansion, CUDOS—known for its distributed GPU compute and decentralized cloud infrastructure—joined the Alliance in October 2024, completing a multi-chain integration that included token migration and validator redelegation to the ASI network.

Together, these founding and network members bring decades of applied research, blockchain engineering, and AI product development. Their collective track record includes innovations such as neural-symbolic cognitive architectures (OpenCog Hyperon), multi-agent simulation environments, tokenized data markets, and globally distributed compute systems—each of which now converges into a single, composable stack.

Milestones to date include the launch of ASI-1 Mini (a modular Web3-native LLM), the release of Qwello (a symbolic research engine), and the deployment of interoperable infrastructure for data, compute, agents, and knowledge. These components power a growing portfolio of tools, agents, and services available to developers and organizations within the ASI ecosystem.

The final phase of the migration—transitioning the $FET token to the formal ASI ticker—is pending. Once complete, this will mark the full realization of a unified token architecture underpinning the entire ASI Alliance ecosystem. This evolution cements ASI as the leading force in decentralized AI, aligning compute, data, agents, and governance into a single, powerful framework for building Artificial Superintelligence.

Whether you’re contributing to open-source infrastructure, running decentralized compute, building AI agents, or staking for governance—$FET is your entry point to the future of AGI, built collaboratively, openly, and at global scale.

Token Migration Guide

Everything you need to know about the multi-phase token merger—conversion rates, timelines, and instructions for migrating AGIX, OCEAN, CUDOS, and FET into the unified ASI token.

What is the ASI token merger?

The ASI token merger unifies four tokens—FET (Fetch.ai), AGIX (SingularityNET), OCEAN (Ocean Protocol), and CUDOS—into a single token: ASI. This consolidation aims to streamline the decentralized AI ecosystem, enhancing interoperability and collective growth.

What are the conversion rates for each token to ASI?

The fixed conversion rates are as follows:​

  • 1 FET = 1 ASI

  • 1 AGIX = 0.433350 ASI

  • 1 OCEAN = 0.433226 ASI

  • 118.344 CUDOS = 1 ASI (after a 5% merger fee)

How do I convert my AGIX or OCEAN tokens into FET (ASI)?

To convert your tokens, follow these guidelines:

  • Cardano AGIX holders: Use the Cardano Migration DApp.
  • Ethereum AGIX holders: Use the Ethereum Migration DApp by SingularityDAO.
  • For step-by-step instructions, check out this Ethereum migration tutorial video.

Do I have to pay any fees to migrate my tokens?

Yes, migration typically requires standard blockchain transaction fees (gas fees), which depend on the network used (Ethereum or Cardano). The migration platforms themselves do not impose additional fees.

Why is this merger significant?

By combining resources and communities, the merger strengthens the infrastructure for decentralized AI, AGI, and ASI development. It also expands the utility of the ASI token across various platforms and services within the alliance.

What are the conversion rates for each token to ASI?

  • 1 FET = 1 ASI

  • 1 AGIX = 0.433350 ASI

  • 1 OCEAN = 0.433226 ASI

  • 118.344 CUDOS = 1 ASI (after a 5% merger fee)

Is there a fee associated with the CUDOS to ASI conversion?

Yes, a 5% token merge fee is applied to CUDOS conversions. This fee supports marketing, research, development, integration, and legal expenses.

When will FET convert to ASI in phase 2?

FET tokens will automatically convert to ASI during Phase 2 of the merger. The date is TBD. 

What are the phases of the token merger?

The merger is divided into two phases:​

  • Phase 1 (Starting July 1, 2024): Rebranding to ASI, merging AGIX and OCEAN into FET, and initiating the conversion process.​

  • Phase 2 (Date TBD): Deployment of the ASI token across multiple blockchains, network upgrades, and finalizing the conversion of all tokens to ASI.

What actions are required from token holders during these phases?

Token holders should:​

  • Monitor announcements from the ASI Alliance for updates.​

  • Use the SingularityDAO platform for conversions, if holding AGIX, OCEAN, or CUDOS.​

  • Ensure tokens are in self-custody wallets if they wish to manually convert them.

Will there be a vesting period for the converted tokens?

Yes, the vesting periods are:​

  • Public CUDOS holders: 3-month linear vesting

  • CUDOS treasury allocations: 10-month vesting

  • AGIX and OCEAN holders: Follow their original vesting terms, now applied to ASI​

How can I convert my tokens to ASI?

The conversion process varies depending on the token:​

  • CUDOS: ERC-20 CUDOS tokens can be converted to FET (ASI) via the SingularityDAO platform.​

  • AGIX and OCEAN: Token holders can convert their tokens to FET using the SingularityDAO dApp.​docs.superintelligence.io+2Fetch.ai+2Ocean Protocol+2

  • FET: FET tokens will automatically convert to ASI during Phase 2 of the merger.​

How can I migrate my tokens?

You can migrate your tokens via SingularityDAO here: https://singularitydao.ai/migrate-asi

Token Merge Process:

  • Token Exchange:
    • CUDOS token holders will exchange their tokens for FET (ASI) at a conversion rate of 118.344 CUDOS to 1 FET.
  • 5% Fee:
    • A 5% token merge fee for marketing, research, development, integration, and legal expenses. This fee is already included in the conversion rate.
  • Vesting Period:
    • Tokens will be linearly vested over a 3-month period.
  • Conversion Process:
    • ERC-20 based CUDOS tokens can be converted to FET (ASI) via the SingularityDAO platform.
  • Network Upgrade:
    • The CUDOS mainnet will be halted.
    • All token balances, including delegated tokens, will be merged into the Fetch (ASI) Mainnet.
    • Delegators not migrating to Fetch.ai (ASI) mainnet will have their funds redelegated to CUDOS-managed validators.
  • Exchange-held Funds:
    • CUDOS holders on exchanges/DEXs may transfer funds to self-custody wallets before the mainnet halt and token delisting.
    • Once in self-custody, holders can convert through the SingularityDAO portal or wait for the network upgrade.
  • Post-merger:
    • Upon completion, the CUDOS token will cease to exist, with its value and utility fully merged into the ASI ecosystem.

Phase 2: ASI Deployment & Network Upgrade – Date TBD
